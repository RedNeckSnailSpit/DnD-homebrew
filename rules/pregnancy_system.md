# Pregnancy System - Homebrew Rule

## When This Applies
After fade-to-black intimate encounters between a fertile male and fertile female character (PC, NPC, or PC-NPC).

## Conception Check
Roll 1d20 immediately after the encounter (DM rolls secretly for NPCs):

**Base DC: 15** (30% chance of conception - rolls of 15-20 succeed)

### Modifiers to DC:
- **-5**: Magic/potion specifically to increase fertility
- **-2**: Certain races with high fertility (halflings, humans)
- **+5**: Magic/potion specifically to prevent conception
- **+2**: Certain races with low fertility (elves, dragonborn)
- **+3**: Stressful conditions (active adventuring, recent combat, poor nutrition)

**If roll meets or exceeds DC:** Conception occurs

## Pregnancy Duration by Race
- **Humans, Half-elves, Half-orcs:** 9 months (38 weeks)
- **Dwarves:** 11 months (48 weeks)
- **Elves:** 12 months (52 weeks)
- **Gnomes, Halflings:** 8 months (35 weeks)
- **Tieflings:** 10 months (43 weeks)
- **Dragonborn:** 8 months total (4 months gestation + 4 months egg incubation, 35 weeks)
  * Egg is laid at 4 months
  * Egg requires consistent warmth (near fire, heated room, or incubated by parent)
  * Egg takes damage as a fragile object (AC 10, 5 HP)
  * Cold environments (below 50°F) require DC 12 Constitution save daily or egg dies
  * Egg hatches after 4 months if properly maintained
- **Other races:** DM discretion

## Pregnancy Stages

**Note:** Trimester lengths scale with race pregnancy duration. Divide total duration by 3 for each trimester.

### First Trimester (Weeks 1 to 1/3 of term)
- **Physical effects:** Mild nausea, fatigue
- **Mechanical effects:** 
  - Disadvantage on Constitution saves vs exhaustion
  - Can be hidden with loose clothing
- **Detection:** DC 20 Medicine check to confirm pregnancy

### Second Trimester (Weeks 1/3 to 2/3 of term)
- **Physical effects:** Visible pregnancy, increased appetite, energy returns
- **Mechanical effects:**
  - No longer can hide pregnancy
  - Disadvantage on Stealth checks (physical profile)
  - -5 ft movement speed
- **Risks:** Moderate physical activity generally safe

### Third Trimester (Weeks 2/3 to term)
- **Physical effects:** Significant size, frequent discomfort, reduced mobility
- **Mechanical effects:**
  - Disadvantage on Acrobatics checks
  - -10 ft movement speed
  - Disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws
  - Cannot wear most armor (requires adjustment)
  - Heavy physical exertion requires DC 15 Constitution save or gain exhaustion
- **Risks:** Combat/adventuring highly dangerous

## Combat and Adventuring While Pregnant

### Damage to Pregnant Character
- **Any damage that reduces character below half HP:** Requires DC 12 Constitution save or miscarriage risk
- **Critical hits to torso:** Automatic DC 15 Constitution save or miscarriage risk
- **Ongoing pregnancy after damage:** DC 10 Medicine check to assess baby's health

### Miscarriage
On failed save:
- Roll 1d20: 
  - 1-5: Miscarriage occurs
  - 6-15: Serious complications, bedrest required (1d4 weeks)
  - 16-20: Baby survives, but continued risk (disadvantage on future saves)

## Birth

### Labor and Delivery
- Occurs naturally at end of term OR can be triggered early by extreme stress/injury
- Requires safe location and time (minimum 1d4+2 hours for uncomplicated birth)
- DC 15 Medicine check by attendant (or DC 20 if self-delivering)
  - **Success:** Healthy baby, mother takes 1 level of exhaustion
  - **Failure:** Complications, mother takes 2 levels of exhaustion and 2d10 damage
  - **Critical Failure (nat 1):** Severe complications, requires magical healing or death save

### Recovery
- **Minimum recovery time:** 2 weeks before adventuring
- **Full recovery:** 6-8 weeks
- During recovery: Disadvantage on Strength and Constitution checks

## Magic and Pregnancy

### Helpful Magic
- **Lesser Restoration:** Removes nausea and minor discomforts (temporary, 8 hours)
- **Greater Restoration:** Treats complications, prevents miscarriage
- **Heal/Regenerate:** Can save mother/baby from critical birth complications
- **Aura of Life/Vitality:** Provides advantage on all pregnancy-related saves while active

### Harmful Effects
- **Polymorph/True Polymorph:** Ends pregnancy if form is incompatible
- **Teleportation (first trimester):** DC 10 Constitution save or miscarriage risk
- **Teleportation (later):** DC 15 Constitution save or premature labor
- **Being aged/de-aged:** DM discretion on effects

## Optional: Magical Contraception
- **Potion of Prevention:** 1 week effectiveness, +5 to conception DC, 25 gp
- **Herbal Remedy:** 1 day effectiveness, +3 to conception DC, 5 gp, requires Herbalism Kit
- **Magical Ward:** Cast by cleric/wizard, 1 month effectiveness, +10 to conception DC, costs spell slot

## Interracial Breeding

### Half-Races (Standard)
When parents are different races, offspring possibilities:

**Human + Elf = Half-Elf**
- 50/50 chance of half-elf traits
- Uses half-elf racial stats
- Pregnancy duration: Average of both parents' terms (10.5 months)

**Human + Orc = Half-Orc**
- 50/50 chance of half-orc traits
- Uses half-orc racial stats  
- Pregnancy duration: 9 months (human standard)

**Other Combinations:**
- DM discretion on whether "half-races" exist
- Generally: Use closest parent race or create custom traits
- Pregnancy duration: Average of both parents, round up

### Dragonborn Exception
**Dragonborn blood is DOMINANT**

- **One Dragonborn parent = Dragonborn child** (always)
- Child inherits dragonborn racial traits in full
- No "half-dragonborn" exists
- Draconic heritage overrides all other racial traits
- Pregnancy follows dragonborn gestation rules (4 months + 4 month egg)
- Child may have minor cosmetic traits from non-dragonborn parent (eye color, build) but is mechanically full dragonborn

### Fertility Between Races
Some race combinations have reduced fertility:
- **Elf + Dwarf:** +3 to conception DC (difficult but possible)
- **Dragonborn + any race:** -2 to conception DC (dragonborn are highly fertile)
- **Gnome + non-Gnome/Halfling:** +2 to conception DC
- **Other unusual pairings:** DM discretion, typically +2 to +5 DC

## Story Considerations
- NPCs may have strong cultural/personal views on pregnancy and family
- Pregnancy can create interesting roleplay opportunities and character development
- DM should discuss comfort levels with pregnancy content in Session 0
- Player can always choose to have NPC raise child, time-skip, or other narrative solutions
- Pregnancy does not need to be a "punishment" - it's a story element

## DM Notes
- Roll conception checks secretly unless player specifically wants to know
- Be sensitive to player comfort with this content
- Pregnancy can be a plot hook, character development, or just background detail
- Don't use pregnancy to "punish" players for romantic RP
- Offer narrative outs (time skips, NPC caretakers, etc.) if player doesn't want to engage

---

**Remember:** This is meant to add realism and potential story depth, not to punish players for character relationships. Handle sensitively and adjust to table comfort levels.
