# Rules

House rules and supplemental systems for D&D 5e. These are designed to add realism, narrative depth, and mechanical support for mature themes at tables that want them.

---

## ⚠️ Content Warning

This directory contains rules dealing with **mature and sensitive themes**, including age of consent, sexual activity (fade-to-black), pregnancy, childbirth, and permanent injury. All systems include their own DM guidance and Session Zero recommendations.

**These rules are entirely optional.** They are designed for adult tables where all players have given informed consent. If in doubt, leave them out.

---

## Index

### [Age of Maturity Guide](./age_of_maturity_guide.md)
A comprehensive breakdown of biological maturity, social adulthood, and legal age by race across the Forgotten Realms — covering everything from drinking ages to marriage laws to cross-species relationships.

Addresses the reality that a 15-year-old human and a 15-year-old elf are at completely different life stages, and how cosmopolitan cities, small towns, and race-specific establishments handle this differently. Includes a quick reference table and roleplay/plot hook suggestions.

> ⚠️ **Sensitive themes:** age of consent, cross-species relationships, marriage law. Includes an explicit golden rule: both partners must be adults by their own race's standards. Intended to add worldbuilding depth, not to create uncomfortable situations.

---

### [Pregnancy System](./pregnancy_system.md)
An optional mechanical system for conception, pregnancy, and childbirth in D&D 5e. Covers conception checks with racial modifiers, trimester-by-trimester mechanical effects, combat risks, miscarriage, labour and delivery, magical contraception, and interracial breeding (including the note that dragonborn blood is dominant).

> ⚠️ **Sensitive themes:** pregnancy, miscarriage, childbirth complications, sexual activity (fade-to-black assumed). Includes explicit DM guidance on handling this content sensitively and offering narrative outs. **Discuss at Session Zero.**

---

### [Rule of Cool](./rule_of_cool.md)
A framework for allowing creative, cinematic actions that fall outside the standard rules — with appropriate checks, consequences, and limits to prevent abuse.

> 📝 **Authorship note:** The "Rule of Cool" is a long-standing piece of D&D community wisdom that predates this document. No single original source is known. This writeup is a personal formalisation of that shared concept, included here for table reference. If you know the original source, please open an issue so credit can be given.
