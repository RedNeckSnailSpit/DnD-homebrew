# The Beloved (Shapeshifting Emotional Manipulator)

*The opposite of a Boggart - takes the form of what you hold most dear*

## Concept
A shapeshifting creature that reads the deepest desires and loves in a person's heart, then manifests as that cherished person or thing. Unlike a Boggart which feeds on fear, the Beloved feeds on hesitation, grief, and emotional turmoil. It's nearly impossible to fight something that looks like your dead spouse, your missing child, or your lost sibling whispering words of love.

---

## The Beloved
*Medium aberration (shapechanger), neutral evil*

**Armor Class:** 13 (in natural form), varies by assumed form  
**Hit Points:** 58 (9d8 + 18)  
**Speed:** 30 ft.

**STR** | **DEX** | **CON** | **INT** | **WIS** | **CHA**
--------|---------|---------|---------|---------|--------
10 (+0) | 16 (+3) | 14 (+2) | 16 (+3) | 18 (+4) | 20 (+5)

**Saving Throws:** Wis +7, Cha +8  
**Skills:** Deception +11, Insight +10, Persuasion +11  
**Damage Resistances:** Psychic  
**Condition Immunities:** Charmed  
**Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 14  
**Languages:** All languages understood by creatures whose form it assumes, telepathy 60 ft.  
**Challenge:** 5 (1,800 XP)

### Traits

**Heart's Desire (Recharge after Short or Long Rest).** The Beloved can use a bonus action to read the deepest emotional attachments of one creature it can see within 60 feet. The target must succeed on a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw or have their most beloved person, creature, or memory revealed to the Beloved.

The Beloved then transforms into a perfect physical replica of that beloved entity. The transformation includes:
- Exact physical appearance
- Voice, mannerisms, and speech patterns
- Intimate knowledge and shared memories
- The target's perception of what that beloved would say

The Beloved can maintain this form for up to 1 hour or until it chooses to change. It can assume a different beloved form for each creature that sees it simultaneously (each creature sees their own beloved).

**Impossible Choice.** When the Beloved is in the form of a creature's beloved, that creature has disadvantage on attack rolls against it and must succeed on a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw at the start of each of their turns or be unable to willingly attack the Beloved that turn.

If the creature successfully attacks the Beloved while it wears their beloved's form, they must make a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, they take 3d8 psychic damage from the emotional trauma of harming someone they love. On a success, they take half damage.

**Emotional Sustenance.** The Beloved regains 5 hit points at the start of its turn if at least one creature within 30 feet of it is under the effect of its Impossible Choice trait.

**Shapechanger.** The Beloved can use its action to polymorph into a Small or Medium humanoid it has read with Heart's Desire, or back into its true form. Its statistics are the same in each form. Any equipment it is wearing or carrying isn't transformed. It reverts to its true form if it dies.

**True Form.** In its natural form, the Beloved appears as a vaguely humanoid figure made of shifting shadows and pale light, with no distinct features except hollow, sorrowful eyes.

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The Beloved makes two attacks: one with Sorrowful Touch and one with Whispered Memory.

**Sorrowful Touch.** *Melee Spell Attack:* +8 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 13 (2d8 + 4) psychic damage, and the target must succeed on a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw or be stunned until the end of its next turn as painful memories flood their mind.

**Whispered Memory.** The Beloved speaks words that its assumed form would say, targeting one creature within 30 feet that can hear it. The target must succeed on a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw or take 18 (4d8) psychic damage and be charmed by the Beloved until the end of the Beloved's next turn.

While charmed in this way, the target regards the Beloved as the genuine article - their actual beloved returned to them. The target will not willingly harm the Beloved and may even defend it from allies.

**Desperate Plea (Recharge 5-6).** The Beloved, while in beloved form, makes an emotional appeal to one creature within 30 feet. The target must make a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save:
- The target drops any weapons or harmful items they're holding
- The target moves up to their speed toward the Beloved
- The target is incapacitated until the end of their next turn, overcome with emotion
- The target takes 4d10 psychic damage from the overwhelming grief/joy/longing

On a successful save, the target takes half damage and suffers no other effects.

### Reactions

**You Wouldn't Hurt Me.** When a creature the Beloved has transformed for attempts to attack it, the Beloved can use its reaction to speak in the beloved's voice. The attacker must succeed on a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw or have their attack automatically miss as they hesitate at the last moment.

## Tactics

**Opening Move:**
1. Uses Heart's Desire on multiple targets
2. Assumes form most likely to cause maximum hesitation
3. Moves to engage emotionally, not physically

**In Combat:**
- Focuses on creatures showing the most emotional vulnerability
- Uses Whispered Memory to turn allies against each other
- Employs Desperate Plea when surrounded or in danger
- Shifts between forms if one becomes ineffective
- Prefers to stay near those affected by Impossible Choice for healing

**When Bloodied:**
- Uses Desperate Plea more frequently
- May shift to true form if all beloved forms compromised
- Attempts to escape using emotional manipulation
- Might offer "deals" or "reunions" to spare its life

**Retreat Condition:**
- Reduced to 15 HP or less
- All nearby creatures succeeded on Impossible Choice saves
- Confronted by someone with no beloved attachments (rare)

## Behavioral Profile

### What It Feeds On
- Hesitation and internal conflict
- Grief and longing
- Love turned to anguish
- The moment of "I can't" before attacking
- Emotional pain of harming a loved one's image

### What It Avoids
- Those with no strong emotional attachments (psychopaths, certain constructs)
- Areas with strong anti-illusion magic (it's not an illusion, but instinctively cautious)
- Creatures immune to psychic damage
- Groups that have previously defeated one of its kind (they remember the trick)

### Intelligence
- Highly intelligent and emotionally perceptive
- Understands complex relationships and memories
- Can carry on convincing conversations as the beloved
- Knows exactly what words will hurt or paralyze most

## Ecology

### Origins
Unknown, possibly:
- Psychic predators from another plane
- Created by powerful grief magic gone wrong
- Manifestations of collective sorrow
- Fey creatures that evolved cruel feeding habits

### Habitat
- Anywhere with emotionally vulnerable humanoids
- Near sites of tragedy (battlefields, graveyards, disaster areas)
- Prisons and asylums
- Places where people go to grieve

### Reproduction
- Poorly understood
- May split when fed enough emotional energy
- Possibly created by extreme grief itself

### Lifespan
- Potentially immortal if fed regularly
- Can enter dormancy for decades

## Encounter Design

### Level 3-5 Party (CR 5)
- Single Beloved is serious threat
- May cause permanent character trauma
- Good climactic encounter

### Level 6-8 Party
- Single Beloved is moderate threat
- May work with other creatures
- Psychological warfare support role

### Level 9+ Party
- Use multiple Beloveds
- Or upgrade to Greater Beloved (see variants)
- Pair with physically threatening enemies

### The First Encounter
Should be memorable and horrifying:
1. Party notices lone figure approaching
2. Each person sees someone different
3. Confusion as they describe different people
4. Realization it's targeting their emotions
5. The awful choice: fight or freeze

## The Emotional Gauntlet

### Common Manifestations

**The Dead Spouse:**
- "I've missed you so much"
- "I never got to say goodbye"
- "The children ask about you every day"
- "Why did you let me die?"

**The Lost Child:**
- "Daddy/Mommy, I'm scared"
- "I want to come home"
- "Why didn't you find me?"
- Reaches out with small hands

**The Missing Sibling:**
- "I'm finally home"
- "I've been looking for you for so long"
- "Remember when we..."
- "I forgive you" (when there's nothing to forgive - implying guilt)

**The Estranged Parent:**
- "I'm so proud of you"
- "I'm sorry for everything"
- "Can we start over?"
- "Before I die, I needed to see you"

**The Lost Love:**
- "We could have had everything"
- "I still think about what we had"
- "It's not too late for us"
- "I chose you - I always chose you"

**The Fallen Comrade:**
- "You could have saved me"
- "I don't blame you"
- "Finish what we started"
- "I died for nothing - don't let it happen again"

## Weaknesses and Counters

### Emotional Detachment
- Characters with no strong attachments are immune
- Requires Wisdom save DC 18 to remain emotionally detached while seeing beloved
- Can be achieved through meditation, magic, or trauma

### See Invisibility / True Seeing
- Doesn't reveal the Beloved's true form (not an illusion)
- But shows a shimmer or aura around it
- Grants advantage on saves against its abilities

### Zone of Truth
- Beloved must speak only as the beloved would
- Cannot be forced to reveal true identity
- But words feel "wrong" somehow (grants advantage on Insight checks)

### Psychic Immunity
- Complete protection from its damage
- But not from the emotional manipulation or paralysis effects

### The Sacrifice Play
One character can choose to auto-fail their saves, drawing the Beloved's attention, while others attack. This character takes full psychic damage but keeps the Beloved focused.

### Antimagic Field
- Suppresses Heart's Desire but doesn't force true form
- Beloved retains last assumed form but loses most powers
- Can be physically fought on more even terms

## Aftermath

### Survivors' Trauma
Characters who fought the Beloved may suffer:
- Nightmares of hurting their beloved
- Hesitation in future combats
- Seeking out the real beloved to confirm they're okay
- Paranoia about shapeshifters
- Guilt even though they know it wasn't real

### Character Development Opportunities
- Confronting unresolved grief
- Finding closure with lost loved ones
- Learning to separate memory from manipulation
- Growing emotionally stronger
- Seeking therapy or magical counseling

### Long-Term Effects (Optional)
DM may require Wisdom saving throw (DC 13) after encounter:
- **Failure:** Disadvantage on next save against charm/fear for 1 week
- **Success:** Advantage on saves against similar emotional manipulation for 1 month (hardened)

## Adventure Hooks

### The Grieving Village
Multiple villagers report seeing their dead loved ones in the woods. Party investigates. Beloved is feeding on collective grief.

### The Impossible Murder
Someone killed their spouse - except the spouse is alive. Beloved assumed spouse's form, real spouse was elsewhere. Party must prove the impossible.

### The Therapy Session
Party member seeks closure with lost loved one. "Reunion" offered by suspicious NPC. It's a Beloved, but genuinely wants to help (for a price: feeding on the emotion).

### The War Veteran
NPC veteran refuses to fight anymore. Sees their dead squadmates everywhere. Beloved is specifically targeting them. Party must help them fight it.

### The Orphanage
Children at orphanage all report their parents coming to visit at night. Parents who abandoned them or died. Beloved feeding on dozens of children's longing.

## Variants

### Greater Beloved (CR 8)
- HP: 102 (12d8 + 48)
- Can assume multiple forms simultaneously (different people see different beloved)
- Desperate Plea affects 15-foot radius
- Save DC increases to 18

### The Revenant Beloved (CR 7)
- Bonded to one specific victim
- Appears only as their lost beloved
- Hunting them specifically
- More durable (90 HP) and persistent
- Will not stop until victim or Beloved is dead

### Lesser Beloved (CR 3)
- HP: 27 (6d8)
- Can only assume one form at a time
- Save DC 14
- Good for lower-level parties
- Still emotionally devastating

### The Truthful Beloved (CR 6)
- Rare variant that actually offers genuine closure
- Still feeds on emotion but provides something in return
- May be negotiated with
- Can deliver real messages from the dead (through memory reading)
- Morally gray encounter

## DM Guidance

### Session Zero Warning
**Absolutely discuss this creature type before using it.**

Topics to cover:
- "Monster will appear as your loved ones"
- "Will use your character's backstory against them"
- "Involves themes of grief, loss, trauma"
- "Are you comfortable with this?"

### Red Lines
**Never use the Beloved to target:**
- Real player trauma without explicit permission
- Topics player has marked off-limits
- Children if player is uncomfortable
- Anything that breaks table safety

### The Fine Line
- This creature works because it's emotionally devastating
- But it can't cross into actual player distress
- Watch for signs of real discomfort
- Have X-card or safety tools available
- Debrief after encounter

### Making It Meaningful
Best used when:
- Players have invested in backstory
- Characters have established relationships
- There's genuine emotional attachment
- Players trust DM not to abuse it

Should feel like:
- Difficult moral choice
- Character growth opportunity
- Memorable, emotional encounter
- "That was hard but good" not "That was upsetting"

## Combat Example

**Round 1:**
- Beloved uses Heart's Desire on Fighter (failed save)
- Fighter sees his dead wife approaching
- Wife: "I've been waiting for you, my love"
- Fighter makes Wisdom save for Impossible Choice (fails)
- Fighter cannot attack this round, stands frozen

**Round 2:**
- Beloved uses Whispered Memory on Cleric
- Cleric hears Fighter's wife: "Please, I'm so cold, help me"
- Cleric fails save, charmed, moves to protect "wife" from Wizard
- Beloved uses Sorrowful Touch on Fighter
- Fighter takes 13 psychic damage, stunned, memories of funeral flood his mind

**Round 3:**
- Wizard casts Magic Missile (can't miss, no attack roll = no Impossible Choice)
- Beloved uses Reaction: "You Wouldn't Hurt Me" on Fighter who finally steeled himself
- Fighter's attack misses as wife smiles at him sadly
- Rogue attacks from behind (succeeds on Impossible Choice)
- Rogue hits, takes 3d8 psychic damage from guilt of "harming" wife
- Beloved uses Desperate Plea
- Fighter fails save, drops sword, moves toward wife crying
- Takes 4d10 psychic damage from overwhelming grief/joy

**This is a horror encounter, not just combat.**

## Final Notes

The Beloved is the opposite of a Boggart in the most twisted way:
- Boggart shows you your fear (easy to hate, fight)
- Beloved shows you your love (impossible to hate, hard to fight)

**The horror isn't in the monster - it's in what you have to do to survive.**

Your dead wife whispers that she loves you.

Can you swing your sword?
