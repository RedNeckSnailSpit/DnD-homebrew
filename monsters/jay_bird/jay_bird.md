# Jay Bird (Charming Murder Bird)

*Inspired by VLDL D&D*

## Concept
Adorable, charming birds that seem harmless alone but become terrifying in swarms. Their charm effect lures victims into complacency before the flock descends to peck away fingers, toes, ears, and eyes.

---

## Jay Bird (Individual)
*Tiny beast, unaligned*

**Armor Class:** 14 (natural agility)  
**Hit Points:** 2 (1d4)  
**Speed:** 15 ft., fly 120 ft., swim 0 ft.

**STR** | **DEX** | **CON** | **INT** | **WIS** | **CHA**
--------|---------|---------|---------|---------|--------
3 (-4)  | 18 (+4) | 8 (-1)  | 2 (-4)  | 12 (+1) | 16 (+3)

**Skills:** Deception +5  
**Senses:** Passive Perception 11  
**Languages:** —  
**Challenge:** 1/8 (25 XP)

### Traits

**Disarming Charm.** When a creature that can see the jay bird starts its turn within 30 feet of it, the jay bird can force that creature to make a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw, unless the jay bird is incapacitated. On a failed save, the creature is charmed by the jay bird for 1 minute. A charmed creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success.

A charmed creature regards the jay bird as adorable and harmless, and will not willingly attack it. The creature can act normally otherwise, but views the bird as "too cute to hurt."

If the jay bird or its companions damage the creature, the charm effect ends immediately.

**Flyby.** The jay bird doesn't provoke opportunity attacks when it flies out of an enemy's reach.

### Actions

**Peck.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 1 piercing damage.

### Tactics
- Jay birds alone are nearly harmless
- They use charm to prevent attacks
- They flee if actually threatened
- Lone birds often scout for the flock

## Jay Bird Swarm
*Medium swarm of Tiny beasts, unaligned*

**Armor Class:** 15 (swarm agility)  
**Hit Points:** 36 (8d8)  
**Speed:** 15 ft., fly 120 ft., swim 0 ft.

**STR** | **DEX** | **CON** | **INT** | **WIS** | **CHA**
--------|---------|---------|---------|---------|--------
6 (-2)  | 20 (+5) | 10 (+0) | 2 (-4)  | 12 (+1) | 18 (+4)

**Skills:** Deception +6  
**Damage Resistances:** Bludgeoning, Piercing, Slashing  
**Condition Immunities:** Charmed, Frightened, Grappled, Paralyzed, Petrified, Prone, Restrained, Stunned  
**Senses:** Passive Perception 11  
**Languages:** —  
**Challenge:** 2 (450 XP)

### Traits

**Overwhelming Charm.** When a creature that can see the swarm starts its turn within 30 feet of it, the swarm can force that creature to make a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw, unless the swarm is incapacitated. On a failed save, the creature is charmed by the swarm for 1 minute. A charmed creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success.

A charmed creature regards the swarm as adorable and harmless. The creature has disadvantage on attack rolls against the swarm while charmed.

If the swarm deals damage to the creature, it can immediately repeat the saving throw, ending the effect on itself on a success.

**Swarm.** The swarm can occupy another creature's space and vice versa, and the swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Tiny bird. The swarm can't regain hit points or gain temporary hit points.

**Flyby.** The swarm doesn't provoke opportunity attacks when it flies out of an enemy's reach.

### Actions

**Frenzied Pecking.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +7 to hit, reach 0 ft., one target in the swarm's space. *Hit:* 14 (4d6) piercing damage, or 7 (2d6) piercing damage if the swarm has half of its hit points or fewer.

**Maiming Peck (Recharge 5-6).** The swarm makes a frenzied pecking attack. If the attack hits, the target must make a DC 13 Dexterity saving throw. On a failed save, roll 1d20:
- **1-2:** The target loses a minor appendage (roll on Appendage Loss table below)
- **3-20:** No additional effect beyond normal damage

### Appendage Loss Table
Roll 1d8 to determine what is lost:

| d8 | Appendage Lost | Effect |
|----|----------------|--------|
| 1  | **Finger** | Disadvantage on Sleight of Hand checks with that hand. If all fingers on one hand are lost, cannot use that hand to hold objects. |
| 2  | **Toe** | -5 ft. movement speed. If 3+ toes lost on one foot, movement speed halved. |
| 3  | **Ear** | Disadvantage on Perception checks relying on hearing. If both ears lost, deafened. |
| 4  | **Eye** | Disadvantage on Perception checks relying on sight and ranged attack rolls. If both eyes lost, blinded. |
| 5  | **Nose** (part of) | Disadvantage on Perception checks relying on smell. Cosmetic damage. |
| 6  | **Tongue** (part of) | Disadvantage on Persuasion and Deception checks. Difficulty with speech. |
| 7  | **Earlobe** | Cosmetic damage only. Cannot wear earrings on that ear. |
| 8  | **Fingernail** | Painful but minor. Grows back in 1d4 weeks. |

**Permanent Damage:** Lost appendages do not grow back naturally. Requires *Regenerate* spell or similar powerful magic to restore.

### Tactics
- Swarm surrounds target, occupying their space
- Uses charm to reduce resistance
- Focuses on isolated targets
- If reduced below half HP, splits into two smaller swarms or flees
- Targets soft, vulnerable areas (faces, hands)

## Encounter Design

### Solo Jay Bird
- **CR 1/8** - Mostly a curiosity or warning sign
- Players might try to catch it or make it a pet
- Lone bird often means a swarm is nearby
- Good for foreshadowing the danger

### Small Flock (2-4 Jay Birds)
- **CR 1/4** - Annoyance level
- Can distract party while other threats approach
- Multiple charm attempts
- Might coalesce into a swarm if threatened

### Jay Bird Swarm
- **CR 2** - Serious threat for low-level parties
- Can permanently maim characters
- Scary moment when the "cute birds" reveal their danger
- Multiple swarms are DEADLY

### Multiple Swarms (2-3)
- **CR 5-6** - Extremely dangerous
- Can cause multiple permanent injuries
- Party might flee rather than fight
- Good climactic encounter for wilderness arc

## Ecology

### Habitat
- Forests and woodlands
- Often near water sources
- Nest in large groups (50-200 birds)
- Territorial during nesting season

### Behavior
- Omnivorous (prefer meat)
- Hunt in coordinated swarms
- Charm effect evolved to lure prey
- Will strip a downed creature to the bone in minutes
- Not inherently evil, just hungry and opportunistic

### Reproduction
- Lay 3-5 eggs per pair
- Eggs are sky blue with silver specks (beautiful and valuable: 10 gp each)
- Fiercely protective of nests
- Young are charming from hatching

### Intelligence
- Simple pack hunters
- Can recognize individual threats
- Remember locations where they've been injured
- Avoid areas with strong magical wards

## Adventure Hooks

### The Cute Companion
Party finds injured jay bird. If they take it with them, it leads its flock to them when they camp.

### The Maimed Merchant
Merchant missing fingers warns party about "adorable birds" in the forest ahead. Party might not believe until too late.

### Nest Raid
Collector wants jay bird eggs for their beauty. Party must navigate territory full of protective swarms.

### The Feeding Frenzy
Party stumbles upon swarms feeding on a corpse. Birds are distracted but aggressive if interrupted.

### Controlled Swarms
Druid or ranger has trained jay bird swarms as guardians. Party must negotiate or find way past without fighting.

## Loot

### From Individual Birds
- Feathers (beautiful iridescent blue-silver, 1 sp each, 2d4 per bird)
- Nothing else of value

### From Swarm
- Feathers (1 gp worth per swarm)
- Occasionally shiny objects in gullet (trinkets, coins, small gems: 2d10 gp worth)

### From Nest
- Eggs (10 gp each, fragile)
- Accumulated shinies (previous victims' jewelry: 4d10 gp worth)
- Small bones (grim reminder of their diet)

## DM Notes

### The Horror Reveal
- Start encounter with ONE adorable bird
- Let players coo over it, try to befriend it
- Maybe have it "play" with them
- Then reveal the flock descending from the trees
- THEN roll for appendage loss
- Players will never trust cute things again

### Balancing the Maiming
- Appendage loss is PERMANENT and SCARY
- Use sparingly - maybe 1-2 times per campaign
- Offers Regenerate spell as quest reward if party member maimed
- Can be great character development moment
- Some players might embrace the injury as character flavor

### Making it Memorable
- Describe the charm effect: "You've never seen anything so adorable"
- The contrast between cute and deadly
- The horror of watching fingers disappear
- Survivor NPCs with missing pieces
- Party paranoia around small birds forever after

## Variants

### Greater Jay Bird Swarm (CR 4)
- HP: 72 (16d8)
- Damage: 21 (6d6) or 10 (3d6) when bloodied
- Maiming Peck triggers on 1-3 (15% chance)
- DC 15 for saves

### Dire Jay Bird (CR 1)
- Large individual bird (unusual mutation)
- HP: 22 (4d8 + 4)
- Damage: 1d6 + 4 piercing
- Still charming, but doesn't need swarm
- Acts as alpha for normal swarms

### Winter Jay Birds
- White and silver coloration
- Cold resistance
- Found in snowy climates
- Even more aggressive (hunger-driven)
